Introduction of 3D Printing Software

3D printing is the process of making 3D solid objects using a digital design. In 3D printing materials such as liquid molecules or powder, grains are joined/fused/solidified together under computer control to create a 3D object. It is basically the process of putting the binder material or binding agent (substance or material that is used to hold or draw other materials) on a powder bed using an inkjet printer head one layer at a time.

3D printing is an additive manufacturing method and therefore, it could replace the subtractive manufacturing method where plastic or metal was cut or hollowed into pieces, like milling machines.

3D printing allows the creation of complex designs without using more materials than traditional manufacturing methods.

The Main features of 3D Printing:

  • Complex Geometries
  • Mass Customization
  • Less Tooling
  • Fewer Costs
  • Environmentally Friendly
