Introduction to the VFX Course

Visual Effects (VFX) is a popular term that describes images created, modified, or enhanced in any film or other motion media that isn't taking place during live-action filming. VFX usually involves the integration of actual footage and manipulated images to create realistic-looking settings for the environment. The environments created are usually unsafe to actually shoot, or they simply don't exist. They make use of computer-generated images (CGI) and specific software for a VFX to achieve this. VFX producers work with cinematographers and directors in order to determine which movies require to create green screens.

Visual effects differ from special effects as a visual effect requires a computer, and is added after shooting. Special effects, also known as SFX, are produced on location. They are the things to know to create like controlled and purposeful explosions as well as fake gunshot wounds and so on.

A prime example for a VFX could be the dragons that fly through the skies from Game of Thrones or a spaceship that flies across space, as seen in Star Wars.
