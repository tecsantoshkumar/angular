Introduction to STAAD Pro Training

STAAD is an abbreviation of Structural Analysis and Design. STAAD Pro is one most popular software which is used to analyze and design structures such as towers, bridges, buildings as well as industrial, transportation as well as utility buildings. Analysis and Design can include any of the construction structures such as tunnels, bridges, culverts, piles, petrochemical facilities; and building materials such as concrete, timber cold-formed steel, and aluminum. STAAD is also known as STAAD Pro was created through Research Engineers International at Yorba Linda, CA in 1997.

Features of STAAD Pro Training

Model generation: The generation of an interactive menu-driven model using concurrent 3D display, 2D and 3D graphics generated by using polar or rectangular coordinate systems. These are segments of repetitive geometry are used to build complex structural models.

Model Verification: 3D/2D drawings displayed on the screen, and printer/plotter 3D models for frames and elements. Isometric or any other rotations to allow complete 3D rendering.

Static Analysis: 3D/2D analyses based on the most advanced Matrix technique to manage huge work. Linear, non-linear, and p-delta analysis using automatic load and stiffness correction.

Dynamic/Seismic Analysis: Mass modeling frequency, mode, and shape response spectrum extraction, analysis of time-history Modal damping coefficient for each model.

Second Analysis: Flexible element capabilities such as designs for concrete and steel and design for timber. Forces and displacements in points between nodes.
